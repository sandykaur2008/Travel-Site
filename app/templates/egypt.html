Egypt

Egypt was a destination I had been dying to visit for many, many years. My childhood friend is Egytian and has family in Alexandria, so we'd grown up discussing this trip and were excited when we were finally able to do it together.

Our first few days were spent in Luxor, visiting ancient sites like the Hatshepsut and Karnak temples, the latter of which we saw at night, when there is an impressive light show in several languages! It was pretty crazy to think of all the mighty pharaohs that had these temples built in their name. I couldn't help but wonder what it must have been like to have all that power...

As my friend is fluent in Arabic, we were fortunate enough to befriend locals. For example, we befriended a local boatman who took us out onto the Nile and helped us get tickets for a hot air balloon tour (which allowed us to see Hatshepsut from above)! He was also kind enough to welcome us to his home and host us for dinner. The hospitality we experienced was truly something else.

After Luxor, we spent time with my friend's family in Alexandria. Her family was incredibly sweet and hospitable. They took us around all over, including to the Citadel of Qaitbay, the Alexandrian library, and to get tea by the sea!

We spent our final days in Cairo. Aside from the pyramids (of course), we visited a bunch of other sites, including Tahrir Square, which has the Egyptian Museum nearby. It's a pretty huge place with a crazy number of ancient artifacts.

The most memorable site of Cairo for me was the Mosque of Muhammad Ali, in the Saladin Citadel. It was gorgeous and overlooked the city, so the views were stunning! I could have stayed there forever.

The Pyramids of Giza were as impressive as I imagined they would be. I rode a camel to get there and it was honestly dreamlike. We also got to see the Sphinx, which was smaller than I thought it would be but impressive nonetheless.
