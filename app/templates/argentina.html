Argentina

I visited Argentina with a friend who has family in Buenos Aires. My friend's cousin lives in an apartment in the Caballito neighborhood, right next to the Parque Centenario. Since we went in October, it was spring in Argentina and lots of people would sit on the grass in the park and drink matte.

Our first few days in Buenos Aires centered on getting to know the lay of the land. We visited many neighborhoods, like Microcentro, Recoleta, and La Boca. The city is gorgeous and has Jacaranda trees all over the place.

La Boca has the colorful Caminito, which was one of the first things I researched once I knew I would be going to Argentina. I couldn't wait to see it!

Microcentro has numerous buildings of note, like Casa Rosada, which houses the office of the President. It was a generally impressive place.

Recoleta was really interesting - it has an awesome museum of waterworks called Palacio de las Aguas, where I got to learn about the history of water distribution in the city. I'd never before been to a museum with so many toilets! Recoleta also has the famous Recoleta cemetary, with mausoleums for such famous people as Eva Peron. It was very beautiful and peaceful.

One of the last things we did in Argentina was bask in the sun at El Tigre. We had lunch by the water - I gorged on steak and fries. *cue Homer drool*

The very last thing of note we did was visit El Ateneo, the massive bookshop in Buenos Aires. I made sure to buy a few books so I could brag I had shopped there.
