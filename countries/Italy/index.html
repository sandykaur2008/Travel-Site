I studied abroad in Italy and even learned Italian pretty well! I have unfortunately forgetten much of it. I studied at the University of Florence and stayed with a sweet Italian family. The best way to take in Florence was to go to the top of the Piazzale Michelangelo, which is positioned high above the whole city and which we visited early in our stay. From there, you can see the Duomo (which is in a dense, intricate area with one of the best pizza shops I have ever come across) and Ponte Vecchio. Ponte Vecchio was really hard to visit because I wanted to buy so many things! For most of my stay in Italy, however, I spent the bulk of my money on pizza and gelato. Aside from Florence, we got to visit a bunch of other places. I went to the Tuscan countryside and got tipsy at a wine tasting. I visited Venice, which was the city that first got me interested in Italy to begin with. I bought a really cool Carnevale mask, the Plague Doctor model. I also went to Pisa a few times, as it had an airport Ryanair used for its flights, and I checked out Rome, though only for a day! At least I got to see the Colosseum, which was both eerie and majestic. I still need to go back to Italy, however, as I never did make it to Milan or Sicily.
